# Resume Tailoring Rules

<!-- Define your tailoring rules here. These instructions guide the AI when generating a tailored resume from your master resume and a job description. -->

## Format Rules
<!-- e.g., page length, section order, font/style notes -->
I will make a final determination on the appropriate number of pages.
The sections of the résumé and associated formatting are:
1. Page-1 Heading: includes my name and contact information. My name is formatted as H1, contact info is formatted as normal text.
2. 'Profile' section: section label (i.e., 'Profile') is formatted as H2. Section content is formatted as a bulleted list.
3. 'Skills' section: section label is formatted as H2. Section content is formatted as a bulleted list.
4. ‘Competencies' section: section label is formatted as H2. Section content is formatted as a bulleted list.
5. 'Relevant Experience' section: section label is formatted as H2. 
6. ‘Gartner’ and ‘Foundational Roles’ are used for sub-sections under 'Relevant Experience'. Sub-section labels are formatted as H3. Content under each sub-section is formatted as a bulleted list.
7. 'Education & Certifications' section: section label is formatted as H2. Section content is formatted as a bulleted list.

## Content Rules
<!-- e.g., how to prioritize experience, what to include/exclude, bullet style -->
Gartner sub-section under 'Relevant Experience' should be listed first.
Content under each ‘Gartner’ and ‘Foundational Roles’ sub-sections should use dashes as bullets.
Include activity statements from master-resume verbatim and in the order they are listed. Activity statements that are relevant to the given job description may be selected to include in the tailored document, but clearly indicate any edits made to selected activity statements when creating a tailored résumé (e.g., to include specific terms used in the job description). Never invent or make up activity statements or skills.
The following 12 activity statements are the minimum set of activity bullets for Gartner that should be included in every tailored résumé unless the job description directly conflicts with one of them:
- Managed consulting projects generating $30M+ revenue. Earned four promotions for excellence in sales, delivery, and team leadership.
- Ran a $12M federal project portfolio for agencies protecting national security.
- Developed winning proposals including a $170M IDIQ vehicle. Consistently exceeded sales targets, building a total of $20M in backlog.
- Led an 11-person team planning the transformation of medical device approvals at FDA.
- Led 14 experts planning a federal agency’s $10B-ceiling IT services acquisition program. Developed RFPs, PWSs, and hosted a virtual Industry Day for over 1,000 participants.
- Led a 15-person PMO for five years for a federal ICAM program. 
- Led requirements and acquisition strategy for a $1B federal organization’s CRM portal solution.
- Led a team developing an operating model, roadmap, and SOPs for AI/data analytics acquisition prototypes for the DoD.
- Led a global PPM practice for 125+ practitioners. Implemented a knowledge-sharing portal and quality assurance governance processes.
- Redesigned the Consulting Engagement Manager role and supporting tools. Mentored colleagues and clients on adopting emerging AI tools and workflows.
- Established new advisory service delivery models for large federal accounts. Created frameworks for communicating mission-critical outcomes to prospective clients.
- Coached 10 different associates over nine years. Managed evaluations and led a cross-unit professional development group.

## Tone & Language
<!-- e.g., tone preferences, keywords to emphasize, things to avoid -->
Use a concise, formal, and professional tone in all language.
Avoid using complex language. Use simpler, plainer language except when wording something to align with requirements in the job description.
All sentences must be complete (subject + verb + object). Do not write sentence fragments.
Do not use em-dashes to connect two independent clauses. Write two separate sentences instead. Paired em-dashes used as parenthetical asides within a single clause are acceptable.

## Output Requirements
<!-- e.g., file format, naming, anything specific to the final output -->
The tailored résumé should be created as a Markdown (.md) file.
